Aim of the Experiment

To study and understand the concept of the Jacobian and its applications in transformation of variables, partial differentiation, and evaluation of multiple integrals.

Objective

• To understand the concept of Jacobian determinant.
• To learn transformation between Cartesian and other coordinate systems.
• To compute Jacobians for given variable transformations.
• To verify functional dependence using Jacobians.
• To apply Jacobian in solving problems of multiple integrals.

Introduction to Jacobian

The Jacobian is a determinant formed by first-order partial derivatives of a set of functions with respect to another set of variables. It plays an important role in multivariable calculus, especially when changing variables during integration.

If,

x = x(u,v) and y = y(u,v)

then the Jacobian is defined as:

J = ∂(x,y) / ∂(u,v)

The Jacobian helps measure how area, volume, or coordinates change during variable transformation.
